# The Xactimate Bottleneck: How to Triple Your Estimator's Throughput

Every day an estimate sits on your estimator's desk is a day you aren't getting paid. Most restoration shops have a "Dead Zone" of 5–10 days between the tech leaving the job site and the estimate hitting the carrier. If you have $200k in "unbilled" mitigation work sitting in your pipeline, your business is effectively a bank giving interest-free loans to insurance companies.

## Why Your Estimators Are Burned Out

Your estimators are likely spending 40% of their time chasing techs for photos or moisture logs. They aren't "estimating"; they are "investigating." This is a systems failure, not a personnel failure. When the field-to-estimate workflow is broken, your estimators can only handle a fraction of the jobs they should be processing.

## The Field-to-Estimate "Dead Zone"

The "Dead Zone" is the friction between field data and office software. If a tech takes photos in CompanyCam but the estimator has to manually download and re-upload them to JobNimbus or Dash, you are wasting labor hours on data entry.

### Eliminating Manual Data Re-Entry
